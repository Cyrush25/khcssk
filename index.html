Saran Swarup Kochhar, a name that resonates with excellence and expertise in the realms of homoeopathy and psychology, has carved an illustrious path throughout his remarkable career. With a multifaceted educational background and an extensive range of professional experiences, Dr. Kochhar stands as a luminary figure in the field, celebrated for his contributions, achievements, and unwavering dedication.
Born on the 8th of January, 1949, in Tumsar, Maharashtra, Dr. Kochhar's academic journey commenced with a strong foundation in science. He secured an impressive 80% in the HSSC from Tumsar and continued his academic pursuits, obtaining a BSc Part - I from Nagpur with a commendable score of 68%. His pursuit of knowledge led him to excel in various domains, including medicine, psychology, and homoeopathy.
His notable achievements include a Diploma in Medicine & Surgery (DMS) from Calcutta Homoeopathic Medical College, where he not only passed with honors but also earned the prestigious Gold Medal, a testament to his exceptional dedication and proficiency. He further expanded his academic prowess with a BA in Psychology from Nagpur University, followed by an MA in Clinical Psychology, wherein he stood first in Order of Merit in the Faculty of Social Sciences.
Intrigued by the intricate facets of the human mind, Dr. Kochhar delved into the realm of psychology, culminating in a PhD awarded by Nagpur University. His research on "Defense Mechanisms & Personality Dimensions" showcased his profound insights into the human psyche, solidifying his reputation as a distinguished psychologist.
Dr. Kochhar's journey seamlessly intertwined with homoeopathy, a field where he not only practiced but also became an exceptional educator. His MD (Hom) in Organon & Homoeopathic Philosophy from Sri Sai Nath Post Graduate Institute of Homoeopathy, Allahabad, was a testament to his commitment to holistic healing. He embarked on a path of both practicing and teaching homoeopathy, sharing his knowledge and expertise with countless aspiring practitioners.
His teaching pursuits extended to various esteemed institutions, including the Nagpur Homoeopathic Medical College, where he served as an Honorary Lecturer. Dr. Kochhar's influence reached beyond the borders of traditional education, as he became a recognized PG guide for Psychiatry by the Central Council of Homoeopathy, New Delhi.
Dr. Kochhar's contribution to research and academia is truly noteworthy. His involvement as a UGC Research Fellow and Doctoral Researcher in Clinical Psychology underscored his commitment to advancing knowledge in the field. He furthered his influence through roles such as a member of the Working Group on Drug Research & Standardization, Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Govt. of India.
In recognition of his exceptional contributions, Dr. Kochhar received numerous awards, including the Rainbow Homoeo Award and the Hahnemann Homoeo Service Award, among others. These accolades stand as a testament to his dedication, expertise, and remarkable impact on the field.
